Overview

Enventure Foundations is designed to provide new entrepreneurs with an understanding of the innovation process and teach them how to evaluate a life science technlogy as the basis for starting a new business. We teach business funamentals and the essential components a tech startup requires in the life science industry

Speakers

Topics

Innovation

Do you think like an entrepreneur? Here we'll discuss how to actively observe your environment for clinical needs which can be adressed with marketable solutions. We'll also consider active brainstorming methods, useful for when the entrepreneur already has a pain point but not the solution. For those of you with too many ideas and not enough time, we'll discuss how to narrow down concepts to the one with the best value proposition.

Idea or Company?

You have a great idea! But how do you know if you can build a company from it? Here, we discuss how to define and target the customers in your target market. What do your customers really need, and are those needs being met already? We'll also discuss how to map out the competitive landscape, how to disrupt that landscape, and how you yourself could be disrupted. Finally, we'll identify key the key stakeholders and their needs, as well as how those needs may be in direct conflict with your other stakeholders.

Business Fundamentals

Sure, you can rip out a spleen or peform a co-IP, but idea of a P&L sheet makes your head spin. We'll go over common corporate structures and what that means in terms of equity. How do you pay people, and how much equity do you give your earliest employees? We'll also discuss how to make a simple budget based on key milestones, and what that means in terms of how long you have until you run out of money. Finally, we'll discuss how to value and early-stage venture, before any profits have been made at all.

Startup Fundamentals

So you've heard of milestones, but what does that really mean? Here we'll go over how to define those key milestones that create the most value for your company and for your investors. Speaking of, we'll discuss the most common sources of funding for your fledgling company, which can come in non-dilutive (think crowdfunding or grants) and dilutive (think angel investors or venture capital). Most startups have venture funding at one stage or another, so we'll discuss how much capital you really need and how those deals are structured.

Pitching

Before you ever sell to a single customer, you'll have to sell your startup to cofounders, investors, and potential employees. We'll discuss the key elements of a great pitch whether it is a one-liner, elevator pitch, or a full slide deck for investors. How do you distill your company into the most important points? What are common pitfalls? And how do you generate a fear of missing out (FOMO)?
