practical skills for life science entrepreneurs

foundations 2015

Overview

free + open course, monthly 6-7:30p @BRC

Enventure Foundations is designed to provide new entrepreneurs with an understanding of the innovation process and teach them how to evaluate a life science technlogy as the basis for starting a new business. We teach business funamentals and the essential components a tech startup requires in the life science industry. Learn how you can turn your creativity loose to design the future of healthcare. Whether you're an engineer, scientist, physician, or business expert, this class will bring you up to speed on the medical innovation industry.

Class Format
35 min lecture
20 min case study
15 min discussion

Topics

Innovation

Do you think like an entrepreneur? Here we'll discuss how to actively observe your environment for clinical needs which can be adressed with marketable solutions. We'll also consider active brainstorming methods, useful for when the entrepreneur already has a pain point but not the solution. For those of you with too many ideas and not enough time, we'll discuss how to narrow down concepts to the one with the best value proposition.

Speaker: Collin Brack, MBA

May 26

Startup Fundamentals

Sure, you can rip out a spleen or peform a co-IP, but idea of a P&L sheet makes your head spin. We'll go over common corporate structures and what that means in terms of equity. How do you pay people, and how much equity do you give your earliest employees? We'll also discuss how to make a simple budget based on key milestones, and what that means in terms of how long you have until you run out of money. Finally, we'll discuss how to value and early-stage venture, before any profits have been made at all.

Bryan Haardt, MBA

June

Startup Deals

So you've heard of milestones, but what does that really mean? Here we'll go over how to define those key milestones that create the most value for your company and for your investors. Speaking of, we'll discuss the most common sources of funding for your fledgling company, which can come in non-dilutive (think crowdfunding or grants) and dilutive (think angel investors or venture capital). Most startups have venture funding at one stage or another, so we'll discuss how much capital you really need and how those deals are structured.

Gray Hancock, MBA

July

Idea or Company?

You have a great idea! But how do you know if you can build a company from it? Here, we discuss how to define and target the customers in your target market. What do your customers really need, and are those needs being met already? We'll also discuss how to map out the competitive landscape, how to disrupt that landscape, and how you yourself could be disrupted. Finally, we'll identify key the key stakeholders and their needs, as well as how those needs may be in direct conflict with your other stakeholders.

Speaker: To be announced

August

Pitching

Before you ever sell to a single customer, you'll have to sell your startup to cofounders, investors, and potential employees. We'll discuss the key elements of a great pitch whether it is a one-liner, elevator pitch, or a full slide deck for investors. How do you distill your company into the most important points? What are common pitfalls? And how do you generate a fear of missing out (FOMO)?

Speaker: To be announced

September

Industry Overview

What are the biggest topics in life science entrepreneuship? We'll bring you up to date on the state of big pharma, biotech, medical devices and diagnostics, and health IT. We'll also look forward to the future and try to figure out what the biggest opportunities are just over the horizon.

Speaker: To be announced

October

Speakers

Speakers for Foundations have been selected from the pool of local entrepreneurs in and around the Houston area, having seen their ability to mentor startups firsthand. We believe that these early to mid-career mentors are the best resource for young and aspiring entrepreneurs. These mentors are where we want you all to be in 5-10 years. Our hope is that you make a real and lasting connection to at least one of these lecturers during the Foundations course.

Collin Brack, MBA; President- HLA Data Systems

Collin is a technology executive with over 15 years of experience in technology management, leadership and operations within the Healthcare and Energy sectors. Business development and product strategy advisor to early-stage companies. Advise corporate leadership and entrepreneurs on business development strategies and emerging technology trends that drive the bottom line, including recent shifts to cloud-computing, SaaS revenue models, and data monetization.

Course Directors

Sarah Hein

Sarah is a PhD candidate in Molecular and Cellular Biology at Baylor College of Medicine. Sarah’s current project studies how a past pregnancy can have lasting effects on their likelihood of developing breast cancer, presented at an international Gordon Research Conference. When she’s not in lab, Sarah spends her time working with Enventure to facilitate community resources for entrepreneurship in Houston.

Will Clifton, MD

Will is a startup consultant and life science entrepreneur in Houston. Will earned a degree in Biomedical Engineering from USC where he joined his first startup, commercializing a novel glucose sensor. After making Houston his home in 2007, Will received his MD from Baylor College of Medicine. Currently, Will is the Director of Research and Development for Procyrion, developing a miniature heart assist pump implanted without surgery. Will also consults for seed-stage startups to help them tell their story, including MolecularMatch, NanoLinea, Anaxiom, and Nano3D. Finally, Will is the founder of Enventure, a grass-roots community focused on growing and educating Houston's medical entrepreneur community. Enventure connects young and aspiring entrepreneurs with experienced entrepreneurs and investors to accelerate the growth of Houston's medtech startup scene. Enventure's educational programming and one-on-one mentorship has launched four life science startups in Houston this year.

Joanna Nathan, MS

Joanna is Director of Research & Development at Saranas and Chief Operating Officer and co-founder at NanoLinea. These companies are interventional cardiology medical device startups spun out of collaborations between the Texas Heart Institute and Rice University. Through these startup roles she has designed and conducted pre-clinical trials, developed regulatory and clinical strategies, and taken ideas from proof-of-concept to prototype to product. Joanna received both her B.S. and Master’s in Bioengineering at Rice University in Houston, TX. Joanna is a founder of Enventure, an organization focused on growing and educating Houston’s life science entrepreneurship community.
